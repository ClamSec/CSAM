Password Strength & Cracking

The best way that users can protect their passwords from cracking is to ensure they choose strong passwords. Typically, passwords must contain a combination of mixed-case random letters, digits and symbols. Strong passwords should never be actual words. In addition, strong passwords are at least eight characters long.

A general formula for calculating the complexity of a password is # of possible unique characters ^ how many characters in the password. For example if your password consist of only eight lowercase letters you have 26 ^ 8 or 208,827,064,576 possibilities. Including numbers as well as lowercase letters in your eight character password increases its complexity to 36 ^ 8 or 2,821,109,907,456 possibilities.

Try passwords and see if they meet recommended requirements for complexity.
