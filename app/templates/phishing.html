Social Engineering: Phishing and You [4/6]

Phishing (aka Email Scams): A malicious entity contacts you, masquerading as a legitimate business and asks for information, directs you to a false website that will harvest your information, or otherwise tries to rip you off and exploit you.

Phishing Email Warning Signs

1. Requests for personal information. Facebook (or any legitimate site) won't ask for your personal info, they already have all of it.

2. Generic greeting. Phishing attempts are most often shotgun blasted at a group of targets and thus are rarely personalized.

3. Urgency. Any email that starts with, "DO x NOW OR YOUR ACCOUNT/MONEY/DIGNITY/OPPORTUNITY IS LOST FOREVER!" should immediately be met with suspicion. Scam artists love fear. Fear makes you stupid, pliable, and easy.

4. Forged/fake/"funny" links. It's super easy to make links look like legitimate links. For example this link: http://www.amazon.com does not lead to where you think it would.
